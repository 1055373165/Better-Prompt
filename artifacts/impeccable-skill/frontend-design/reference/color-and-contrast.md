# Color And Contrast

- Build from tinted neutrals first, then add one or two assertive accents.
- Keep surfaces related to the brand hue so the interface feels cohesive even when mostly neutral.
- Prefer warm or cool off-whites over flat `#fff`, and softened ink tones over pure black.
- Use color to define priority, status, and mood, not just decoration.
- Keep text color tuned to the surface beneath it; neutral gray often looks lifeless on colored panels.
- Reserve the strongest accent for key actions, live states, and meaningful highlights.
- Avoid the common AI palette of dark navy, cyan glow, and purple gradients unless the product truly demands it.
